Liver Function Test

A Liver Function Test (LFT) is a blood test designed to assess the overall health and functionality of the liver. It measures various enzymes, proteins, and substances produced by the liver, providing critical insights into its condition and helping to detect potential liver diseases or dysfunctions. Key components typically measured in an LFT include alanine aminotransferase (ALT) and aspartate aminotransferase (AST), which indicate liver damage or inflammation when elevated. Alkaline phosphatase (ALP) levels can suggest bile duct obstruction or liver disease, while bilirubin levels help identify jaundice and liver dysfunction. Additionally, albumin, a protein produced by the liver, is measured, as low levels may indicate liver disease

Instructions

Before undergoing a liver function test (LFT), it is crucial to follow specific instructions for accurate results. Generally, fasting for 8 to 12 hours prior to the test is recommended, allowing only water. Inform your healthcare provider about all medications, supplements, and herbal remedies you are taking, as some may affect the results. It is advisable to avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours beforehand and maintain a normal diet, steering clear of heavy or fatty meals the day prior. Staying hydrated is important, so drink plenty of water unless instructed otherwise. Always follow any additional guidance from your doctor.If you have any existing health conditions, particularly liver-related ones, be sure to discuss them with your doctor. Additionally, it’s essential to communicate if you are pregnant, as this may influence the test procedure.

What to do just after Blood Test

Avoid any side effects after a blood test, you should: Apply Pressure and Keep the Bandage On: This helps prevent bruising and bleeding at the puncture site. Stay Hydrated: Drinking water can help replenish any fluid loss and reduce the risk of dizziness or fainting. Avoid Strenuous Activities: Refrain from heavy lifting or intense exercise for the rest of the day to prevent strain on the puncture site. Rest if Necessary: If you feel lightheaded or weak, sit or lie down until you feel better. Monitor the Puncture Site: Check for any unusual swelling, redness, or pain, and report any concerns to a healthcare professional. By following these steps, you can minimize the risk of side effects after your blood test.
